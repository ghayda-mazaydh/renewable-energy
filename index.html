Theory

The solar cell works in several steps: - Photons in sunlight hit the solar panel and are absorbed by semiconducting materials, such as silicon. - Electrons are excited from their current molecular/atomic orbital. Once excited an electron can either dissipate the energy as heat and return to its orbital or travel through the cell until it reaches an electrode. Current flows through the material to cancel the potential and this electricity is captured. The chemical bonds of the material are vital for this process to work, and usually silicon is used in two layers, one layer being doped with boron, the other phosphorus. These layers have different chemical electric charges and subsequently both drive and direct the current of electrons. - An array of solar cells converts solar energy into a usable amount of direct current (DC) electricity. - An inverter can convert the power to alternating current (AC).
